Chapter 23

_______________________________

Jin’s gaze sharpened. “You’ve seen him before. You remember… don’t you? Just a glimpse. A shadow from your past. The one who waits. Watches. Challenges.”

Kamikaze closed his eyes. Flashes—half-formed memories—surged inside his mind: a cold presence on a rooftop, eyes burning with quiet power, a silhouette in the dark, silent and watching. But the details slipped away before he could grasp them fully.

“Why do I feel like I know him?” Kamikaze whispered.

Jin stepped closer, voice low but fierce. “Because you do. But he is not who you think. And he is more than just the next opponent.”

A long pause.

“You thought Ryujin and Raijin were your greatest challenge,” Jin said, “but Zero is something else entirely. The one who holds the true key to your past… and your future.”

Kamikaze’s fists clenched. His heart thundered with the fire of a thousand battles.

“If I’m to face him,” Kamikaze said, “then I’ll have to unlock more than just my power. I’ll have to unlock my memories. And the truth behind this war.”

Jin nodded once.

“Then prepare. The road to Germany will be the hardest one yet.”
