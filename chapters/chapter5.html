THE TRAINING BEGINS

The morning sun was harsh, burning through the thin clouds as Jin barked out orders. We ran drills that pushed us beyond pain, beyond sanity. My legs screamed for mercy, but my mind was louder — the war had begun, and I had no choice but to answer. Every sprint, every tackle, every shot was a test of will, a battle against my own limits. Pain was no longer an enemy—it was a weapon, a reminder that I was still alive, still fighting. Each day chipped away at the weak parts of me until all that remained was fire.
