Chapter 31

_______________________________

Kamikaze collapsed, his body heavy and broken — but it wasn’t a heart attack.

His soul was ripped free from flesh and bone, flung into a place of pure, endless blackness.

There was no light, no sound—only darkness so complete it swallowed everything whole.

Yet, even in that void, Kamikaze saw.

He saw it all—memories, moments, the path he had traveled.

And then he saw himself.

Not the Kamikaze he knew, but a stranger—his past self, a shadowed figure he’d never met, yet somehow recognized.

That ghostly form hovered before him, dark aura swirling, vast and consuming.

The darkness it emitted was so deep and cold that Kamikaze’s bones trembled, cracking under its weight.

A shiver ran through him like ice shards, splitting his soul apart.

He was face to face with a power buried deep within—something wild, raw, and terrifying.

And as he floated downward into the abyss, he understood:

The dark figure’s voice echoed in the void — low, rough, and unsettling.

“I have no name,” it said, voice like distant thunder rumbling through the blackness.

“Call me... Monster.”

The word hung in the air like a curse, heavy and cold.

Kamikaze’s heart (or what was left of it) trembled.

Monster’s aura pulsed — a deep shadow of raw power and endless rage.

“I am what you fear. What you try to hide,” Monster whispered.

“But I am also your strength. Your darkness forged into a weapon.”

Kamikaze stared into the abyssal eyes of Monster, feeling a storm raging inside himself.

The shattering of his bones wasn’t pain.

It was the breaking of old chains.
