Whispers on the Fields

Chapter 20

_______________________________

The final whistle blew.

No confetti rained.

No gods descended.

Just a sound. A truth.

Kamikaze had done it.

He beat Raijin, graceful as lightning.

He surpassed Ryujin, chaos incarnate.

He scored the winning goal for Japan.

And now?

He sat in silence.

Wrapped in a thick hotel robe, staring at a feast that could feed kings. Wagyu beef. Tuna belly. Golden miso soup. Fluffy rice. Champagne sparkled in crystal glasses.

But Kamikaze didn’t move.

“I thought I’d feel different,” he muttered.

The TV murmured match replays. His teammates screamed in celebration down the hall. Cameras flashed, and somewhere in Tokyo, a parade was forming in his name.

But none of it reached him.

He chewed slowly. Mechanical.

Not satisfied. Not hungry. Just… waiting.

And that’s when he heared it.

A flicker. A gaze. Somewhere far above.

Like a big challege incoming
