Whispers on the Fields

Chapter 24

_______________________________

The roar of the stadium faded into the background as Kamikaze sat on the cold bench, jersey damp with sweat, heart pounding in his chest. The Nationals Cup was over — he had won. But now, in the big league, the Japan National Team, he was once again a substitute. A bencher.

Raijin and Ryujin, his old rivals, prowled the field like gods, playing with their signature contrasting styles — Raijin’s graceful, clean precision like the calm before a storm, Ryujin’s chaotic, dark-matter energy ripping through defenses like a black hole consuming light.

Kamikaze clenched his fists, bitterness rising like fire in his veins. He had poured everything into that victory, unlocked every ounce of potential, yet here he was — sidelined, watching legends play while his name rarely left the coach’s lips.

And yet… in the back of his mind, a shadow stirred. A fleeting memory, barely there — a figure watching the match from a rooftop, eyes piercing, cold. Zero.

Who was he? Why did Kamikaze feel like he’d seen him before — a ghost from the past that refused to be forgotten?

As the cheers thundered around him, Kamikaze gritted his teeth.

“This isn’t over.”

He didn’t know what lay ahead. He only knew one thing — to face Ryujin and Raijin again, to finally prove himself, he would have to confront not just his rivals… but the mystery that haunted him from the shadows.

And when the time came, he would be ready.
