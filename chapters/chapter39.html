Match 4: Japan (30) vs Tunisia (20)

The heat of the midday sun beat down on the pitch as Japan prepared to face Tunisia, ranked 20th. Tunisia was a tougher opponent—solid defense, quick counter-attacks, and a physical style that tested every player’s endurance.

Japan’s team was still disjointed, but Kamikaze was starting to gain respect from his teammates. Today, he was starting on the field as a striker — a small victory.

Right from kickoff, Tunisia pressed hard. Their captain, Wahbi Khazri, directed plays with experience, launching attacks down the wings.

Japan struggled to hold possession, and the crowd’s nervous energy was palpable.

In the 22nd minute, Tunisia scored from a corner kick — a powerful header by Ellyes Skhiri.

Japan was down, and the players’ frustration showed.

But Kamikaze refused to back down.

He used every bit of training, every ounce of the Monster’s strength, weaving through defenders with dazzling dribbles.

In the 39th minute, Kamikaze scored a brilliant goal — a curling shot from outside the box that left the keeper frozen.

The stadium erupted.

Half-time came with the score tied 1-1.

In the second half, the game turned brutal.

Tunisia’s defense tightened, and Japan’s attacks were repeatedly thwarted.

At 70 minutes, Kamikaze was fouled hard, earning a free kick.

With icy focus, he took the kick himself.

The ball sailed over the wall, dipping just under the crossbar—goal!

Japan led 2-1.

For the final 20 minutes, the team fought with everything.

Despite Tunisia’s fierce efforts, Japan held on.

The whistle blew.

Victory.

For Kamikaze, the first real win with the national team.

But the climb was only getting steeper.
