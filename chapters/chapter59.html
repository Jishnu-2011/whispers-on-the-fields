The 100th Match — Kamikaze & Zero vs. Noel Noa

The stadium was silent, the air thick with tension. For 99 matches, Kamikaze and Zero had been relentlessly crushed by the champion Noel Noa — a towering figure whose skills seemed otherworldly, untouchable.

But this match felt different. Kamikaze’s dribbling was sharper, Zero’s passes more precise. They moved like synchronized shadows, weaving through Noa’s defense with daring, relentless attacks.

And then — the unthinkable happened.

Kamikaze, with a burst of speed powered by the monster inside him, dodged past Noa’s defenders and fired a shot that slipped just under Noa’s outstretched hand.

Goal.

The crowd erupted — not in victory, but in awe. It was the first time anyone had ever scored on the undefeated Noa.

Though the scoreboard showed a crushing 1-10 defeat, Kamikaze’s heart soared. This was more than a goal — it was a message. The monster within was waking, and the legend of Kamikaze was only beginning.
