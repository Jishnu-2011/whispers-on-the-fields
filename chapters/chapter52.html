Chapter 52

first match

The stadium was silent except for the muffled echoes of a select few watching the high-stakes clash. Japan’s Kamikaze and Germany’s Zero faced off against Portugal’s Ronaldo and Argentina’s Messi — titans of the game in a fierce 1v1 duo battle.

From the start, Kamikaze unleashed the dark, wild energy of his monster self, sprinting past Ronaldo with blazing speed and dazzling dribbles. Zero matched Messi’s graceful yet deadly attacks with calculated defense, saving shots that seemed impossible to stop.

For nearly the entire match, the score was locked at zero — each side holding their ground, neither willing to give an inch. Kamikaze broke through the defense once, nearly scoring a stunning solo goal, but Ronaldo recovered just in time, blocking the shot with sheer power.

In the final moments, Messi’s magic shone brightest. With a sudden burst of speed and a flawless feint, he sliced through Zero’s defenses and set up Ronaldo for a precise strike.

The ball hit the back of the net.

Japan and Germany’s duo fought to the very end, but the scoreboard read:

Portugal & Argentina — 1

Japan & Germany — 0

Kamikaze’s eyes burned with frustration and hunger — this loss was a crushing lesson, but also a fire to push harder.

The Egoesta Champions League had only just begun, and the road ahead would be even more brutal.
