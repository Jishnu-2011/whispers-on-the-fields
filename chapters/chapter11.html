THE CLASH OF LEGENDS

The first time I stood on the training ground of the Japan National Youth Team, I felt it—pressure thick enough to choke.

There they were.

Ryujin.

Hair like smoke. Eyes like a black hole. His movements: unpredictable, cruel, devouring. Dark matter made human.

Raijin.

The opposite. Golden eyes. Calm footsteps. A stillness before the strike. He moved like music, perfect and untouchable.

They didn’t speak to me.

They didn’t need to.

Their eyes said everything:

"You are nothing."

And yet—I felt no fear.

I burned.

We clashed in a practice match.

Me vs them.

They toyed with me at first. Ryujin’s chaos rattled my timing. Raijin read my every step like a book he'd already memorized.

But then the other me awakened again.

And for a moment—just a blink—I slipped past both.

Split them open with a strike that cracked the crossbar.

The field went silent.

Then Ryujin laughed.

“You think this is the summit?” he said.

Raijin’s voice followed, ice-cold:

“We were just your test. The real enemy’s inside.”
