THE SHADOW

That night, Jin summoned me. Alone.

His office was dim, the only light a flickering screen showing dozens of player profiles.

He turned to me.

“You’re not the only one with a ghost inside you,” he said.

“Every top player in this world—Raijin, Ryujin, Riku—each of them carries a second soul. But yours… yours is unstable.”

I asked him what he meant.

“You weren’t born with talent, Kamikaze. You stole it. In the wreckage of your collapse, something else moved in. A predator. A remnant of an ancient player—one who was erased from history because he broke the code. He didn’t play for glory. He played to destroy.”

I couldn’t breathe.

Was I even me anymore?

Or just a vessel?

“You can’t beat Ryujin and Raijin by training harder,” Jin said.

“You need to confront the thing inside you. Or it will eat you alive.”
