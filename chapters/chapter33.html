Whispers on the Fields

Chapter 33

_______________________________

With Monster by his side, Kamikaze unlocked a new level of skill — lightning-fast reflexes, razor-sharp dribbling, and an uncanny ability to read the field like a chess master. On the pitch, he became a tempest, weaving through defenders with impossible grace and power.

But outside his own brilliance, the Japan team was still a sinking ship — plagued by weak passes, sloppy defense, and a lack of cohesion that made them the worst of the worst at the World Cup.

Kamikaze’s dazzling dribbles and explosive plays dazzled crowds and opponents alike, but no matter how hard he fought, his teammates couldn’t keep up. It was like running a race alone, dragging a broken cart behind him.

He surpassed everyone — not just Ryujin or Raijin anymore — but even his own team. They relied on him as a one-man army, but the weight of their failures pressed on him heavier than ever.

Every goal he scored was a flash of brilliance in a dark storm.

Every victory was bittersweet, because deep down, he knew: no matter how much he grew, the team was still falling apart around him.

And that loneliness? It fueled his rage and determination even more.

If he was going to carry Japan, he’d become unstoppable.
