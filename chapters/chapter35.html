Chapter 35

_______________________________

The road ahead was merciless.

Forty-eight teams. Forty-eight battles.

Each one tougher than the last.

Kamikaze knew this wasn’t just a tournament — it was a war for honor, pride, and survival.

Every match was a mountain he had to climb alone, carrying the weight of a team too weak to keep pace.

But he didn’t flinch.

With Monster by his side, and the fire in his veins, he vowed to fight each battle with everything he had.

From ruthless defenders in Brazil to cunning midfielders in Spain, from aggressive forwards in France to tactical giants in Germany — every opponent was a wall he had to break through.

Every victory brought him closer to the summit, closer to the ultimate goal.

Noel Noa, the world champion, loomed at the top — a shadow waiting to crush him.

But Kamikaze’s eyes burned with a new resolve.

He wasn’t just fighting to win. He was fighting to prove that even the weakest team could rise — if only one warrior dared to carry the flame.
