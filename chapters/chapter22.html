Whispers on the Fields

Chapter 22

_______________________________

The limo cut through Tokyo’s neon streets like a blade through night.

Kamikaze sat silently in the backseat. The World Cup medal hung heavy around his neck, but it felt lighter than the questions in his chest.

He stepped out at the gates of the World Class Football Program. Everything looked smaller now. Not weaker—just… human.

The training halls that once felt like a prison now felt like a graveyard of the old him.

He walked the hallway alone.

No guards.

No screaming coaches.

Just the echo of his boots—and a door at the end, still half-open, as if it had been waiting for him.

Inside, Jin stood by the window.

Same coat.

Same aura of steel and frost.

But this time, when he turned, his eyes were not filled with commands or fury.

They were calm. Studying. Proud? No. Something deeper.

“So,” Jin said. “You won.”

Kamikaze nodded.

“Ryujin. Raijin. Japan. All of it.”

“Was it worth it?” Jin asked.

A pause. Kamikaze looked at his hands. Calloused. Scarred. Still trembling.

“I don’t know.”

Jin smirked faintly.

“You became what I designed… and still you ask questions. That means you surpassed the design.”

Kamikaze stepped closer.

“What now? What was the point of all this? Just to win?"

“No,” Jin said. “To survive. To find the ones who truly exist above the game. You think Zero just watches for fun?”

Kamikaze’s breath caught.

“You know him.”

Jin turned back to the window.

“I created him. Before I created you.”

Silence.

Thunder cracked outside.

Jin finally faced Kamikaze fully.

“If you want peace, walk away now. You’ve earned it. But if you still feel that fire in your blood… if the war still whispers to you…”

He tossed a file on the desk.

It read only one word: GERMANY.

“Then go find Zero. And end this.”

Kamikaze picked up the file.

Eyes burning again. But this time not with doubt.

With resolve.
